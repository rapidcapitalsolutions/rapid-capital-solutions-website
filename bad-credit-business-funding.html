Bad Credit Funding

Business funding when your credit is not perfect

Banks decline files for low FICO, thin credit, or past setbacks. We specialize in revenue-based programs that weigh your deposits and business performance — not just a score.

Apply With Bad Credit Confidential Consultation

Can you get business funding with bad credit?

Yes — many alternative funders underwrite on cash flow first. If your business shows consistent deposits, active operations, and manageable existing debt, you may qualify even when traditional lenders say no. Approval is never guaranteed; each file is reviewed individually.

Best funding options for low credit

  • Merchant cash advance — strongest fit for 500+ FICO with solid revenue; some programs go lower with higher volume
  • Revenue-based term products — fixed daily or weekly payments based on deposits
  • Secured / asset-backed — equipment or real estate may improve terms
  • Lines of credit — typically require 650+ FICO but worth exploring as credit rebuilds

Why banks decline — and what we look at instead

Banks prioritize personal credit, collateral, and industry risk tiers. Alternative funders prioritize:

  • Average monthly gross deposits (often $10K+ minimum)
  • NSF/overdraft frequency on bank statements
  • Time in business and industry type
  • Existing advance positions and payment history

How to improve your approval odds

  1. Connect a clean business checking account with 3–6 months of statements ready
  2. Disclose all existing financing upfront — surprises delay funding
  3. Apply for an amount aligned with your deposit volume (avoid over-leveraging)
  4. Work with a broker who shops multiple funders, not just one lender

Important: We do not promise guaranteed approval. We match your file to funders whose guidelines fit your profile and explain total cost before you accept any offer.
